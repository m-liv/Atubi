Welcome to Mo’orea, French Polynesia!

Background Information

Mo’orea, located in French Polynesia, is a breathtaking island known for its pristine beaches, lush landscapes, and vibrant marine life. The island offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure, with opportunities for snorkeling, hiking, and immersing oneself in Polynesian culture amidst stunning tropical scenery.

Cultural Norms

When traveling to Mo’orea, visitors are encouraged to embrace the warm hospitality of the local people, who deeply value respect and community. It's important to greet others with a friendly "ia ora na" (hello) and "mauruuru" (thank you) to show appreciation for their culture and customs. Respecting traditional protocols, such as removing shoes when entering someone's home or a sacred space, demonstrates reverence for Polynesian traditions. Additionally, visitors should adhere to eco-friendly practices, such as avoiding littering and respecting marine life while snorkeling or swimming in the lagoons.
