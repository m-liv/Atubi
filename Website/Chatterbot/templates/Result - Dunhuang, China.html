Welcome to Dunhuang,China!

Background Information

Dunhuang is situated near the edge of the Gobi Desert and the Taklamakan Desert. It's most renowned for the Mogao Caves, a complex of 492 temples carved into the cliffs that contain some of the finest examples of Buddhist art spanning a period of 1,000 years.

Cultural Norms

In Dunhuang, visitors to temples are expected to dress modestly as a sign of reverence, while adhering to local customs like shoe removal displays consideration for cleanliness and tradition. Social exchanges are characterized by nods or handshakes, reflecting a blend of politeness and formality, particularly in interactions with esteemed individuals. Bargaining in bustling markets is a customary practice, yet it is essential to negotiate with decorum, striving for fair outcomes that honor mutual respect.
