Welcome to Dunhuang,China!

Background Information

Dunhuang is situated near the edge of the Gobi Desert and the Taklamakan Desert. It's most renowned for the Mogao Caves, a complex of 492 temples carved into the cliffs that contain some of the finest examples of Buddhist art spanning a period of 1,000 years. The city's history as a cultural and religious melting pot is evident in its art, architecture, and manuscripts, which incorporate influences from various cultures along the Silk Road.

Cultural Norms

In Dunhuang, echoing broader Chinese cultural values, respect and harmony are deeply cherished. Visitors to temples are expected to dress modestly as a sign of reverence, while adhering to local customs like shoe removal displays consideration for cleanliness and tradition. Social exchanges are characterized by nods or handshakes, reflecting a blend of politeness and formality, particularly in interactions with esteemed individuals. Bargaining in bustling markets is a customary practice, yet it is essential to negotiate with decorum, striving for fair outcomes that honor mutual respect.
